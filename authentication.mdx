## Overview

PRCS uses secure email-based authentication to grant employees access to corporate services. You sign in with your company email and password, with options for verification, password reset, multi-factor authentication (MFA), and secure logout. Follow these procedures to set up and maintain secure access.

<Callout kind="info">
  Always use your official company email address (e.g., `yourname@company.com`) for authentication. PRCS does not support personal email addresses.
</Callout>

## Email Verification Setup

Verify your email during initial registration or after changes to ensure account security.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Request Verification" icon="mail">
    Navigate to the PRCS login page at `https://dashboard.example.com/login`. Enter your email and select "Send Verification Email".
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check Inbox" icon="inbox">
    Open your email client and locate the verification message from `no-reply@prcs.com`. Click the verification link.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm" icon="check-circle">
    Return to PRCS dashboard. Your account status updates to verified.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<ParamField path="email" param-type="string" required="true">
  Your company email address for verification.
</ParamField>

## Password Reset Procedures

Forgot your password? Reset it securely via email.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Web Dashboard" icon="globe">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Initiate Reset">
        On the login page, select "Forgot Password?" and enter your email.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Follow Email Link">
        Check your inbox for a reset link from `security@prcs.com`. It expires in 15 minutes.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Set New Password">
        Enter a strong password (at least 12 characters, including uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols).
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="API Reset" icon="code">
    Use the reset endpoint with your email.

    <CodeGroup tabs="JavaScript,Python">
      ```javascript
      const response = await fetch('https://api.example.com/auth/reset', {
        method: 'POST',
        headers: { 'Content-Type': 'application/json' },
        body: JSON.stringify({ email: 'yourname@company.com' })
      });
      ```
      ```python
      import requests
      response = requests.post('https://api.example.com/auth/reset', json={
        'email': 'yourname@company.com'
      })
      ```
    </CodeGroup>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

<Response tabs="200,400">
```json
{"message": "Reset email sent", "expires_in": "15m"}
```

```json
{"error": "Email not found"}
```
</Response>

## Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) Enablement

Enable MFA for added security using an authenticator app.

<Expandable title="Enable MFA via Dashboard" default-open="true">
  1. Log in to `https://dashboard.example.com`.
  2. Go to Account > Security > MFA.
  3. Scan the QR code with Google Authenticator or Authy.
  4. Enter the 6-digit code to confirm.

  <Callout kind="tip">
    Store backup codes securely. They provide recovery if you lose app access.
  </Callout>
</Expandable>

<ParamField header="Authorization" param-type="string" required="true">
  Bearer token including MFA code, e.g., `Bearer YOUR_TOKEN_123456`.
</ParamField>

## Logout and Session Security

Secure your sessions by logging out after use and managing active sessions.

- Always click "Logout" from the dashboard menu to invalidate your session.
- Review active sessions at `https://dashboard.example.com/account/sessions`.
- Sessions expire after 30 days of inactivity or on logout.

<Callout kind="alert">
  Never share your credentials. Use incognito mode for shared devices and clear browser data after logout.
</Callout>
